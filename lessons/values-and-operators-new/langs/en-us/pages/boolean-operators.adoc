= Boolean Operators

Pyret also has operators that work on _Booleans_. For each expression below, _write down your guess_ about what it will evaluate to. Then type them in and see if you were right!

@vspace{2ex}

[cols="3, 3, 2", stripes="none", frame="none"]
|===

| *Expression*
| *Guess what it will evaluate to*
| *Does Pyret Agree?*

| `(3 <= 4) and (3 == 2)`
| @fitb{20ex}{}
| @fitb{20ex}{}

| `("a" == "b") and (3 <> 4)`
| @fitb{20ex}{}
| @fitb{20ex}{}

| `(3 <= 4) or (3 == 2)`
| @fitb{20ex}{}
| @fitb{20ex}{}

| `("a" == "b") or (3 <> 4)`
| @fitb{20ex}{}
| @fitb{20ex}{}

| `not(10 > 2)`
| @fitb{20ex}{}
| @fitb{20ex}{}

| `not( (3 <= 4) and (3 == 2) )`
| @fitb{20ex}{}
| @fitb{20ex}{}

| `(3 <= 4) and not(3 == 2)`
| @fitb{20ex}{}
| @fitb{20ex}{}

| `not( (3 <= 4) or (3 == 2) )`
| @fitb{20ex}{}
| @fitb{20ex}{}

| `(not(3 <= 4) or (3 == 2))`
| @fitb{20ex}{}
